POUR

P - Perceivable O - Operable U - Understandable R - Robust

Level of success criteria

A - web content must be made accessible in order for individuals with disabilities to access the content at all

AA - web content should be made accessible for wider group of users with disabilities to access content

AAA - describes items on web pages that can be made accessible to allow the widest amount of individuals with disabilities to use the site

Principles of Universal Design 1996

Equitable, flexible use, Simple intuitive use (KISS), Perceptible information, Tolerance to error, Size and space for approach and use,

Design Thinking

Integration and iteration, Divergent thinking, Convergent thinking, Human-centered, Insight (empathy),

ARIA History

Specifications started in 2008 - completed 2014, Html attributes that make web content accessible with Accessible Technology, Semantic html5 (2010) made some aria attributes redundant, Bridges gaps in accessibility that html5 does not cover, No ARIA is better than bad ARIA
